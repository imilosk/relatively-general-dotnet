About Relatively General .NET

Welcome to Relatively General .NET, your curated source for the latest and most insightful articles from the .NET ecosystem. This site aggregates high-quality content from leading .NET blogs and community contributors, bringing you a comprehensive view of what's happening in the world of .NET development.

Why Relatively General?

The name "Relatively General" is a combination of Einstein's theory of relativity and the fact that this blog is generally about .NET development.

Open Source

This project is open source and available on GitHub. We believe in transparency and welcome contributions from the community. Whether you want to suggest new content sources, improve the aggregation algorithms, or enhance the user experience, your contributions are welcome.

Contact Us

Have suggestions for new content sources or features? Want to contribute to the project? Feel free to reach out to us at milos@relativelygeneral.net. We'd love to hear from you!

Content Sources

We aggregate content from respected voices in the .NET community, including official Microsoft blogs, MVPs, and experienced practitioners. All articles link back to their original sources, ensuring proper attribution and supporting the original authors. You can view our complete list of content sources in our GitHub repository.

RSS Feed & Newsletter

Do you offer an RSS feed or a weekly newsletter? Not yet, but these are features we're considering for the future. Stay tuned!

Note: This site aggregates and summarizes content from various sources. We strive to provide accurate summaries and proper attribution. For the complete articles and the most up-to-date information, please visit the original sources.
